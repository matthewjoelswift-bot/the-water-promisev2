How it works

Play. Get faster. Give water.

Three modes, one loop: finish a round, an ad refreshes, a cup gets funded. Here's what each mode does and why it actually helps you.

The typing coach (desktop)

Most typing apps just time you. This one coaches you. Research on 136 million keystrokes found that fast typists don't press keys harder — they smooth out the tiny gaps between keys. So the game watches your timing, finds the exact letters and letter-pairs slowing you down, and builds your next round around them. It keeps you in the proven learning "sweet spot" — around 94% accuracy — nudging difficulty up when you're crisp and easing off when you slip, and it introduces new patterns slowly, the way a musician practices a hard passage. That's deliberate practice, automated.

Why it coaches accuracy before speed

It feels backwards, but the fastest way to get faster is to stop making mistakes, not to mash keys harder. Every typo costs you the error plus the time to notice it and back up — a triple penalty that wrecks your real, corrected speed. That's why the coach holds you near 94% accuracy: high enough that you're clean, low enough that you're always being stretched. When you're crisp it pushes the pace; when you slip it eases off and drills the letters that tripped you. You're not grinding random paragraphs — you're practicing the specific things that are slow for you, which is what actually moves the needle.

The word puzzle (mobile) — the rules

Nobody wants to sprint-type with their thumbs, so phones get a word puzzle instead. You have 6 tries to find a hidden 5-letter word. After each guess, every letter is colored:

Green — right letter, right spot.
Yellow — that letter is in the word, but in a different spot.
Grey — that letter isn't in the word at all.

Use the clues to narrow it down and solve it before you run out of guesses. Solving funds a cup. And it's not just a game: guessing under letter-position constraints is real spelling and letter-pattern practice, and after each solve we show the word's meaning — words you miss come back later, so you actually remember them. (We won't oversell it: it sharpens spelling and recall; it won't magically expand your vocabulary.)

The 1-minute face-off

Type as many words as you can in 60 seconds, then challenge a friend with a link to beat your score. Every finished word funds a cup.

Where the money goes

Finishing a round refreshes the ads; advertisers pay for those views; we donate a share to fund clean water through charity: water. One run funds at least one cup. The full math is on the Impact page.

FAQ

Is real money moving yet?
Not yet — this is a prototype. We're setting up the ad and donation rails and an official charity: water campaign first.
Does my streak carry between my phone and my computer?
Yes — one account, one streak, whether you're typing or solving the word puzzle.
What's a "cup"?
Our simple unit for the water we fund. charity: water turns $1 into ~4,400 cups, so one run funds one real cup's worth of clean water.
Is it free to play?
Completely. The ads fund the water and the site — you never pay anything, and there's nothing to buy to play.
Do I need an account?
No. You can play everything without signing in. An account just saves your stats, streak, and leaderboard name across your devices.
Where do the ads come from?
Standard display advertising (we're setting up Google AdSense). No pop-ups and no autoplay noise — just a banner that refreshes when you finish a round.
