Why milestones and streaks, not points

The milestones aren't just decoration — they're doing a job. Getting faster at typing, like getting better at anything, comes from showing up in small, regular doses rather than one heroic session. So the game is built to reward the habit, not the binge. Your streak follows you across your phone and your keyboard, so a word puzzle on the bus and a typing run at your desk count toward the same unbroken chain. The milestones mark the compounding: ten thousand cups feels far away on day one and inevitable by week three, because a few minutes a day quietly adds up. The same daily habit that carries the water is the one that makes you measurably faster — which is exactly the point.
