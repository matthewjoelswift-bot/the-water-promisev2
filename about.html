About

A global water movement, built one path at a time — and one keystroke at a time.

The Water Promise started as a simple idea: what if a few minutes of a game could turn into real clean water for someone who needs it? We built it around a small, honest loop — play a round, an ad refreshes, and a share of that money funds a cup of water through charity: water.

Then we made the game worth playing on its own. It's a genuine typing coach that finds the keys slowing you down and helps you get faster, plus a word puzzle for your phone and a quick head-to-head. You come back because you're improving; the water gets funded because you came back.

We're deliberate about honesty. "Cups" is our unit for the dollars raised — charity: water does the actual field work, and donations go straight to them. We'll publish what's raised and what's given, and we don't make impact claims we can't back up.

Why a typing game

We tried the idea a few ways before it clicked. The version that stuck was the one where the game is genuinely useful even if you don't care about the cause at all. Typing speed is a rare thing: a skill almost everyone uses every day, that most people never actually train, and that you can measurably improve in a couple of weeks. "Get a little faster today" is a reason to come back tomorrow — and daily return is the whole engine of an ad-funded model. The habit that helps you is the same habit that funds the water.

Why charity: water

If we're going to turn attention into water, we wanted a partner whose work we could point at without flinching. charity: water has spent close to twenty years funding clean-water projects with local organizations, and they publish the proof — GPS coordinates and photos of completed work. Their 100% model means every public donation goes to the field; their operating costs are covered separately by private backers. That's the rare setup where "we raised this much for water" and "this much became water" are the same sentence.

How we stay honest

Honesty here isn't a vibe, it's a set of rules we hold ourselves to. We only count impact we can back with receipts. We keep donations structurally separate from our own costs, so the giving can't quietly get diluted. We say plainly when something is still a prototype (it is, right now) rather than dressing up illustrative numbers as real ones. And when the ad and donation rails are fully live, we'll publish a running ledger of what came in and what went out. The mission only works if people trust it, and trust is easy to lose and slow to earn.

Where this goes

Right now it's small and built in the open. The near-term plan is straightforward: stand up an official charity: water fundraising campaign so giving flows directly to them, wire the ad and payment rails, and switch on the automated donation of our pledged share. After that, the work is the boring, good kind — make the game better, help more people get faster, and let the water grow with the audience.

The Water Promise is a project of The Little Tree Company, LLC. It's early, and it's built in the open. If it grows, the water grows with it.
